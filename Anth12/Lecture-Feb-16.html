Mutation in DNA

  • Bad Mutations
    • Reduction on protein's ability to function
    • In structural or regulatory genes
    • in sex cells
  • Neutral Mutations
    • no change in protein
    • no effect on reproduction
    • In non-coding regions(introns)
  • Good mutations
    • Increases protein's ability to function; enhances fitness

Natural Selection

  • How does Natural Selection work?
    • Natural Selection operates on phenotypes of individuals
    • Evolution operates on populations
    • Environment can affect phenotypes
    • Changes in genes
  • Patterns of Natural Selection
    • Directional Selection
      • Environment affects the growth of different characteristics in species.
    • Stabilizing Selection
      • selection based on the attributes that are considered to be optimum
      • Birth weight is on average around 5lb and that would be considered optimum and a stabilizing selection.
  • Natural Selection in Humans
    • Sickle Cell (malaria)
      • Balanced Polymorphism
        • AS have higher survival rate (immune to Malaria)
    • Thalassemia

Forces of Evolution

  • Other ways By Which Evolution Happens
    • Gene Flow: movement of genes between populations (interbreeding: biological diversity, inbreeding)
    • Genetic Drift: random changes in gene frequency in a population (only genotypes of subpopulation)
      • Extreme weather: hurricane wipes out a gene pool
      • Founder effect: limited allele frequency (population is on an island)
        • Ellis-van Creveld Syndrome (EVC) (Amish)
      • Genetic Bottleneck: elephant seal, limited gene pool
