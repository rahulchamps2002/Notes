Virtue Ethics

The Basics

  • Virtue = "A tendency or disposition, cultivated by habit, to have appropriate beliefs and feelings and to act accordingly" (Tiberius, 2015, p. 109).
  • Lying in the mean - between two extremes
  • Obtained through the "right education" (The good person takes pleasure in doing virtuous acts)
  • Nature AND habit as important components
  • Important for flourishing (the good life)
  • It is difficult to find the mean
    • And we ought to be skeptical of pleasure especially: "Now in everything the pleasant or pleasure..."
  • Connection with Character
    • Within the contemporary literature, virtues are often thought of as being th esame as or similar to character traits
    • When we speak about people behaving morally, we odten appeal to character/make ascriptions of character
    • "Just think while you've been getting down about the liars, and the dirty, dirty cheats of the world..."
  • What are we doing when we make character ascriptions?
    • Explaining: If we know that someone has a particular character trait, and we take chracter traits to be stable across situations, we can predict what they are going to do.
